Unison aggregates protein sequences and sequence fragments from a large number of databases. The intent is to contain a superset of all available sequences. After sequences are loaded, the most promising sequences are identified based on origin, species, and other properties. These sequences are subjected to an array of analyses whose results are stored; results for other sequences will be loaded on request.

The analyses currently include signal sequence and transmembrane predictions, BLAST, HMM (Pfam) alignments, GPI anchoring, and others.
