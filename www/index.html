What is Unison?

Unison is an experiment in integrating a variety of protein sequence information, primarily for the purpose of mining for novel members of protein families.

Unison is unique in the breadth of sequence sources and types of information. Sequences are stored non-redundantly and analyses are "keyed" to those unique sequences (read: you'll never analyze the same thing twice). The precomputed analyses currently include aggregate sequence properties, signal sequence and transmembrane prediction, and alignments from structure based profiles and threading (BLAST and HMM alignemnts are in the works.) All of this is is available via SQL for rapid and complex querying.

Unison was originally created by Reece Hart. It is now an active research project and benefits enormously from the assistance of David Cavanaugh.

Unison is a work in progress and very incomplete. Links on these pages and the code behind them are unstable.
